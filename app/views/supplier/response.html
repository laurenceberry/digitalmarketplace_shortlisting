What happens next

The buyer creates a shortlist

When the opportunity closes the buyer will score your evidence. They’ll create a shortlist of the most suitable suppliers.

It takes at least 3 days for a buyer to create a shortlist and it can take several weeks.

If you're shortlisted

If you’re one of the shortlisted suppliers, you'll go through to the next stage, known as the evaluation stage.

If you're not shotlisted

If you’re not successful, the buyer will let you know and may give you feedback on the evidence you provided.

The buyer evaluates the shortlisted suppliers

At the evaluation stage, the buyer will get in touch to ask you to provide:

  • further evidence of your skills and experience
  • your proposal

Your evidence must describe the skills and experience of the people who’ll be working on the project.

The buyer will use the assessment methods listed in their requirements to evaluate your evidence. They’ll use:

  • interview

The buyer will use the weightings they published with their requirements to score the suppliers who reach the evaluation stage. They’ll provide feedback if you’re unsuccessful.
